## The Unexamined Subject

**Who is the one who suffers?**

Most discussions of suffering focus on its objects — anxiety, regret, fear, conflict, loss. This essay turns attention toward the subject itself. What exactly is the "I" that claims ownership over experience?

Drawing upon insights from Vedanta, Buddhism, Sufism, Sikhism and modern masters like J Krishnamurti, Osho and Prashant, this essay investigates the structure of the ego, the role of conditioning and psychological time, the observer problem, and the possibility of living without inner conflict. Rather than offering a new belief system, it is an invitation to examine the assumptions that underlie our sense of self.

## Na De Dil Pardesi Nu: Love, Self, and the Dissolution of Ego

**A Punjabi folk song as a map of spiritual transformation.**

At first glance, _Na De Dil Pardesi Nu_ appears to be a song about romantic longing. Beneath its surface lies one of the oldest motifs in mystical literature: the Lover and the Beloved.

Through the poetry of Kabir, Meera, Guru Nanak, Guru Gobind Singh, Rumi, and Sufi symbolism, this essay explores the psychological meaning hidden within the song. The poison, the longing, the Jogan, the Nightingale and the Rose — all become metaphors for the dissolution of ego and the emergence of a radically different understanding of love.

## The Collapse of the Borrowed Self
**On identity, loss, and what remains.**

What happens when the foundations upon which a life has been built suddenly disappear? Through the stories of Andrew Laeddis from _Shutter Island_, Bhikkhuni Patachara, Krishna, and Guru Gobind Singh, this essay explores the relationship between identity and suffering.

Moving between psychology, philosophy, Advaita, Buddhism and Sikhism, the essay examines whether there exists a way of living that remains unbroken even when everything else is lost.
